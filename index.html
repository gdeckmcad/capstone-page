It's Accrual World

A choose your own adventure banking game to help educate young Americans on financial health and resilience.

An MCAD MAGWD Capstone Project
Designed by Grace Deck, Summer 2021

THE CONCEPT

Americans are being undermined by chronic instability. Since the 70s banks have become bigger and bigger and are lending less, charging more fees, and increasingly only serving the wealthiest customers. Benefits fall on employee's shoulders, wages have decreased and become volatile, and the cost of health care, childcare, and education keep increasing making it harder and harder to make ends meet.


The financial landscape is complex with bloated jargon, confusing figures and a contentious history. It's Accrual World aims to help young Americans make sense of the financial industry in a choose your own adventure banking game. The game grounds players in character scenarios to build financial health, literacy and resilience as they navigate real-world financial decisions through the characters' lens.

THE INSPIRATION

Largely inspired by how my mom brought this humanness to the cold topic of budgeting for college when I was a teen. She helped me figure out what schools I could afford by walking me through how different tuitions would translate into monthly student loan payments and how that would fit in with different jobs and salaries I might get after graduation. She encouraged me to keep my options open, that accomplishing your dreams can come in different ways and on budget.

01. User flows

To understand the logic of the game, I created user flows for all of the characters to map out their different decision and pain points.

Tools:
Whimsical

02. Wireframes

Next, using the user flows as a screen inventory I moved into wireframing. I went through a couple rounds of wireframes, leaving out visual design at this stage helped me focus on getting my content and structure in a good place without worrying about branding and colors. Once I had created wireframes for each screen I built a clickable prototype in InVision to get ready for user testing.

Tools:
Whimsical
InVision

03. User feedback

I ran two user tests at pivotal points in the project. The first was in the wireframe stage where I conducted 3 moderated tests with my target audience. After the first test I applied insights to visual designs and then surveyed another 5 users in an unmoderated test.

Tools:
Google Forms
InVision

Key themes I was seeing in feedback were:

  1. Users didn't understand the conclusion of the character journey, and wanted to see and compare between the different routes they could've chosen to better understand the takeaway of the game
  2. Users wanted to see a timeline or progress bar of their activity to help them inform their next step
  3. Users felt they weren't understanding the decisions they were making and wanted to feel more invested in their decisions with more explicit content and direction
